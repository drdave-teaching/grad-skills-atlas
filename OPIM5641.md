# OPIM 5641 — Skills Atlas (Business Decision Modeling)

*What you'll be able to do — each skill paired with its theory, its real-world implementation, and **how to practice it**.*

**Legend:** ✋ **by hand** (math/manual) · 💻 **local code** (a notebook) · ☁️ **production** (deployed in the cloud)

**Learning objectives:** ①translate a business problem into a **math program** (decision vars · objective · constraints) ②solve **LPs** graphically & by **Simplex** ③solve & interpret models in **Pyomo** ④read **sensitivity** (shadow prices, reduced costs) ⑤model **nonlinear** problems (portfolio, location, regression-as-opt) ⑥model **networks** (flow, assignment, shortest-path) ⑦model **integer & logical** decisions ⑧quantify **uncertainty** (Monte Carlo)

---

## In one sentence
By the end, **you can turn a messy business decision into a math program, solve it — by hand and in Pyomo — and explain what the optimal answer and its shadow prices actually mean for the business.**
